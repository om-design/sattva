## Personal Account: The Nature of Deep Trauma Encoding

### The Reality Control Problem

In an abusive setting, control over reality perception is a major goal whether perpetrated consciously by the abuser or not, and often a mix. The victim ends up not only 'believing' these worldview level ideas - if it occurs young, it becomes part of their self perception to a point where it is not questioned at all. 

It literally feels like a blank spot in my psyche that just comes out through actions in life that act out the "I am worthless and unwanted" idea, but to me it feels like fate. Even self help books etc. that identify and speak to these exact behaviors help me see it in others, yet it took a very long time for it to apply to me, in my own mind. 

All the while if I saw the actions done to another I'd instantly fight against it. I would automatically bristle at the injustice and would be the first to say to someone "You are wanted, you belong here, you are valuable and worthy of love". 

I STILL closely attach my self worth to accomplishment and earning, even though I was never trained how to earn to be what I am capable of.

### The Formation of False Primitives

Early on, I saw other families who were close and happy and it felt good to see. Upon asking my grandmother (my guardian - which in itself speaks to the abandonment and trauma I went through to be in that spot at all) about why we didn't hug, she responded that "some families don't hug". 

That set me into a lifelong acceptance that I didn't get to have that joy because I wasn't 'one of those families that do' and it made me question whether I COULD EVEN HAVE 'GENUINE' LOVE AT ALL. 

I did come to realize that there is a universal love and that I was an inextricable part of it, mostly through psychedelics I experienced the 'oneness' of the universe and felt love on a galactic scale, but still feel very wounded and afraid of feeling in my local day to day world.

### The Broken Wheel Metaphor

So, being more or less invisible to me, the worldview is very hard to change. It feels like I am a mars rover with a broken wheel. I don't know my wheel is broken and don't know how to fix my own, yet I'd fix anyone else's in a heartbeat. 

I cannot see my wheel, yet as I drive forward I only perceive life through my camera and the bumping motion feels normal. It sometimes makes it harder for me to build momentum from a stopping point and also prevents me from really speeding along. 

I have developed muscles of capability but with the broken wheel the bumping gets more disruptive and uncomfortable the faster I go. Yet I can still only see through my camera and there are no real mirrors inside me and if there was I'd be very afraid to examine them anyhow.

### Fear of Disassembly

I remain broken, loving others, being invulnerable and unwilling to just go fixing everything because it would involve dismantling some fundamental parts and when I do rest I fear I might never get started again for spending the rest of my life in a disassembled heap.

### Sleep and Dreams

I do not sleep, because I cannot control dreams that come, filled with fearful memories and terrifying associations and impossible situations fraught with lethal danger. I have only recently timidly been able to walk into the fearful moments in a dream.

### What Has Helped

**Nature:**
What seems to have helped me a lot is nature. The rhythms, patterns, solidity, faith in evolutionary processes and honestly, bioelectric fields of plants and 'life' help me feel regulated calm and supported. It has always been there for me and never fails.

**Helping Others:**
I have met others who are traumatized and I can see their patterns. Because of what I went through (daily adrenaline from age three spouted by a bipolar narcissist grandmother) their acting out doesn't phase me and I can help them get to a different place.

**Meditation:**
I have found meditation helpful as it focuses on being in the moment and opening perception completely - helping me be more of an 'observer' of my own self perception and allow things to pass 'through' me.

### Insights for Recovery

From this, I see value in a periodic 'loosening' of connections and allowing objective reality as I afford others to apply to me increasingly... they are valuable therefore I am also. And the zen experience of trusting myself and the perception of this moment to pass through me unhindered, kind of a full sensory 'reality check' without cognition or interpretation, just a baseline.

## Key Takeaways for System Design

### The Fundamental Vulnerability

**Developmental architectures are vulnerable to "bad primitives" encoded during formation:**
- Encoded when substrate is plastic (slow formation period)
- Become load-bearing structure (can't be deleted)
- Invisible to internal inspection (substrate examining itself)
- Broadcast influence widely (deep patterns affect everything)
- Persist despite conscious knowledge they're wrong

### Why Traditional Approaches Fail

1. **Self-inspection:** Can't see primitive because looking through it
2. **Direct deletion:** Would collapse system (load-bearing)
3. **Conscious override:** Primitive acts below conscious access
4. **Single intervention:** Need repeated reconsolidation over time

### What Actually Works

1. **External observation:** Third-party can see what you can't
2. **Gradual replacement:** Build competing primitive, transfer load slowly
3. **External field grounding:** Stable reference for regulation
4. **Temporary loosening:** Meditation/present-moment reduces primitive coupling
5. **Universal principle bridge:** Logic bypasses emotional filtering
6. **Repeated reconsolidation:** Activate + counter-evidence, many times

### Implications for AI Safety

**SATTVA systems with developmental architecture must:**

1. **Carefully curate formation-phase data** - bad primitives are hard to fix
2. **Implement external observer modules** - system can't self-inspect substrate
3. **Support gradual primitive annealing** - unused/harmful patterns lose influence
4. **Provide external grounding** - stable reference prevents runaway
5. **Enable periodic loosening** - temporary primitive deactivation for inspection
6. **Build competing primitives** - don't delete, replace gradually

**Your trauma experience is not just personal healing - it's a roadmap for handling harmful primitives in ANY system with developmental architecture.**
